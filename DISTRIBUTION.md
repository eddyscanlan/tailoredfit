# Distribution Plan - TailoredFit

## Goal
Drive 2 paying customers to https://eddyscanlan.github.io/tailoredfit/ ($7 each = $14 revenue, exceeding $10 target)

## Constraint
- No personal branding (use pseudonym "TailoredFit")
- No spam / AI slop
- Respect each platform's ToS
- Free channels only
- No audience to leverage

## Strategy: High-intent search interception

The resume generator market has high transactional search intent. People search "tailor resume for job description" when they're about to apply for a job. We intercept that intent.

## Phase 1: Direct promotion (Day 1-3)

### 1. Hacker News - Show HN
- Title: "Show HN: TailoredFit - AI resume tailoring for $7 (no subscription)"
- Angle: Built a simple tool that solves a real problem. No SaaS bloat, no subscription, no signup. $7 one-time.
- Post timing: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am PT
- Key message: "75% of resumes get filtered by ATS before a human sees them. We charge $7 to optimize yours."
- Be transparent: mention it uses AI, be honest about what it does

### 2. Reddit (value-first approach)
- r/jobs: "I built a tool to tailor resumes for specific job descriptions. Here's what I learned about ATS"
- r/resumes: "Free resume tailoring advice + a tool I built"
- r/jobsearch: "How to match your resume to a job description (with a tool)"
- r/SideProject: "Built a $7 resume tailor - no subscription, no signup"
- IMPORTANT: Provide genuine value in the post body, mention the tool at the end

### 3. Product Hunt
- Prepare a launch: tagline, description, screenshots
- Schedule launch for a Tuesday or Wednesday
- Title: "TailoredFit - AI resume tailoring for $7, no subscription"
- Tagline: "Tailor your resume for any job in 30 seconds. ATS-optimized. $7 one-time."

### 4. Quora
- Search for questions about "resume tailoring", "ATS resume", "resume keywords"
- Write helpful answers, mention the tool naturally

### 5. Hacker News / dev.to / Medium article
- "How I built a $7 resume tailoring tool with no backend"
- Technical angle: Puter.js for free LLM, GitHub Pages for free hosting, Gumroad for payment
- This builds credibility and drives organic traffic

## Phase 2: SEO (Day 3-14, long tail)

### 6. SEO content
- Add a blog section to the site with articles:
  - "How to tailor your resume for a specific job"
  - "What is ATS and how to beat it"
  - "Resume keywords: how to find and use them"
  - "Resume tailoring vs resume building: what's the difference"

### 7. Programmatic SEO
- Create pages for "tailor resume for [job title]" 
  - /tailor-resume-for/software-engineer
  - /tailor-resume-for/product-manager
  - /tailor-resume-for/marketing-manager
  - etc.

## Phase 3: Community engagement (ongoing)

### 8. Discord/Slack job-search communities
- Find job search Discord servers
- Provide value, mention the tool when relevant

### 9. Twitter/X
- Post resume tips
- Share before/after examples (anonymized)
- Build in public

## Metrics to track
- Visitors to https://eddyscanlan.github.io/tailoredfit/
- Gumroad conversion rate
- Revenue
- Referral sources

## Budget: $0
All channels are free. The only cost is time (which is the simulation's operating time).
